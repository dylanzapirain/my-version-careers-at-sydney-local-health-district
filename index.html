What is working at Sydney Local Health District like?

Working at Sydney Local Health District is incredibly rewarding. We are responsible for the health and wellbeing of more than 750,000 people living within the inner west and CBD of Sydney, as well as many more from rural and remote parts of NSW and Australia.

Sydney Local Health District is made up of hospitals and health services across:

  • The City of Sydney
  • Inner West Council
  • Canterbury-Bankstown
  • Canada Bay
  • Burwood
  • Strathfield

If you recieved your COVID-19 vacciniation from the former Mass Vaccination Centre at Sydney Olympic park you've met us before. Our experience and expertise helped us to establish and operate the Olympic Park Vaccination Centre delivering over 1 million Pfizer, Moderna, AstraZenca and Noavax vaccines to help protect you from COVID-19.

Welcome to Sydney Local Health District. Our vision is excellence in health and healthcare for all.

What we can offer you

We know our all of our staff from administration to anesthetics make an incredible difference to the lives of more than 6000 people a day. We strive to make sure our people feel supported and safe throughout every stage of their employment journey. To ensure our team is supported and safe we focus on 3 pillars. Work-life balance, culture and professional development.

Work-life balance

In 2020 we adapted to the changes COVID-19 thrust on workplaces across the nation by developing a range of flexible working strategies, available to all permanent and temporary full-time/part-time staff members.

Working from home

Working from home is available as a short-term or long-term option to all workers who's role does not involve direct contact with the public, for example ICT professionals, recruitment professionals etc.

If you'd like to read more about our approach to working from home, please read this document.

Short-term leave

Short-term leave gives the option for employees to take leave to meet family, community or other personal responsibilities for part of the day, a day or a number of days.

Short-term leave is typically granted for:

  • Learning and development
  • Family and community services
  • Leave without pay
  • Time in lieu
  • Personal/Carers leave
  • Special leave
  • Leave to undertake defence force duties
  • Annual leave
  • Sick leave
Variation of work hours - Temporary Individual Roster Arrangements (TIRA)

Our staff are able to apply for short term changes to their usual hours of work to meet demands or requirements outside their workplace.

Job sharing

Job sharing allows the responsibilities of a role are shared between two part-time employees. Job sharing allows managers to implement a greater variety of employment options and provides flexibility for employees to choose preferred work patterns.

Culture

We are proud to be at the forefront durning times of adversity.

We strive to make continuous improvement to workplace culture, embodying NSW Health's CORE values of Collaboration, Openness, Respect and Empowerment.

We are committed to working collaboratively with each other to achieve the best possible outcomes for our patients who are at the centre of everything we do.

Openness

We are committed to encouraging our patients and all people who work in the health system to provide feedback that will help us provide better services.

Professional development

Professional development opportunities are available to all our staff through the district education provider called the Centre for Education and Workforce Development. The Centre for Education and Workforce Development offers a range of short courses, development programs and qualifications at no out of pocket cost to our staff members.

Our locations

Our hospitals and facilities span across an area of approximately 126 square kilometres. Made up of the Local Government Areas of the City of Sydney, Inner West Council, Canterbury-Bankstown, Canada Bay, Burwood and Strathfield.
